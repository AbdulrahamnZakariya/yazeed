Communication Skills

On the third day of the course, we attended a series of soft skills lectures focused on Communication Skills, sponsored by Ms. Balqis Al-Araj from Orange, an expert in this field.

Ms. Al-Araj conducted engaging activities, starting with the DISC test,
assessing personality traits based on responses to 24 questions.
The test results were categorized into four types, and each group presented one type.
For example, the first group covered the "D" type, known for leadership and preference for quick results.

Additionally, we participated in an interactive exercise, passing a message from one student to another,
demonstrating the importance of clear communication.

Efficiency and Self-Empowerment

Coach Balqis gave a lecture titled "Efficiency and Self-Empowerment" focused on time and task management techniques.
She introduced the SMART goal-setting framework, which helps in organizing and achieving objectives effectively. SMART stands for:

  • Specific: The goal must be clear and precise, answering What, Why, Who, Where, and How?
  • Measurable: Goals should be quantifiable to track progress.
  • Achievable: The goal should be realistic and attainable.
  • Relevant: The goal must align with broader objectives.
  • Time-bound: Every goal should have a deadline for prioritizing time.

We discussed task categorization, dividing tasks into four quadrants: urgent and important,
important but not urgent, urgent but not important,
and neither urgent nor important. This method helps prioritize tasks effectively.



Change Management

In our soft skills session, Coach Balqis explored change management principles,
focusing on structured approaches to transition from a current state to a future state.
We focused on five key change management models:

Change Management Models

  1. Kübler-Ross Change Curve: Describes emotional responses to significant changes through denial,
    anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance.
  2. Kurt Lewin's Change Management Model: A three-step model: Unfreeze, Change, and Refreeze, to implement and embed changes.
  3. McKinsey 7-S Framework: Focuses on seven elements: Strategy, Structure, Systems, Shared Values,
    Skills, Style, and Staff, aligning for successful change.
  4. ADKAR Model: Focuses on Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, and Reinforcement for individual change.
  5. Kotter's 8-Step Change Model: Emphasizes leadership with steps like creating urgency,
    forming coalitions, and embedding change in culture.

We researched and presented each model in groups, gaining a deeper understanding of managing change effectively.



LinkedIn and CV Documentation

1. What is LinkedIn?

LinkedIn is a professional social network for career development, networking, and job searching.

2. LinkedIn Basics

Steps to build a strong LinkedIn profile:

  • Professional Picture: Use a clear, professional image.
  • Headline: Concisely describe your professional identity.
  • Summary: Briefly overview your skills and achievements.
  • Experience: Document your job history with key achievements.
  • Skills: Add relevant skills and seek endorsements.
  • Recommendations: Request recommendations to strengthen your profile.
  • Engage: Interact with relevant content and join professional groups.

3. How to Make Your Profile Job-Ready

  • Keyword Optimization: Use industry-specific keywords.
  • Specialization: Define your professional field clearly.
  • Highlight Achievements: Focus on measurable results.

4. What is a CV?

A CV is a detailed document primarily used in academic or research fields.

5. What is a Resume?

A Resume is a concise document highlighting relevant experience and achievements for non-academic jobs.

6. What is ATS CV?

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) filters resumes for specific keywords and formats. Tips for ATS-friendly CV:

  • Use keywords from the job posting.
  • Avoid complex designs or graphics.
  • Stick to simple text formatting, like bullet points.

Additional Tip: Continuous Updates

Regularly update your LinkedIn and CV to reflect your growth and new skills.
