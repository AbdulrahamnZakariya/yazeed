Uses of Altium Software

  • Designing printed circuit boards (PCBs).
  • Editing and designing electronic circuits.
  • Simulating and testing the functional performance of circuits before manufacturing.
  • Generating manufacturing files for PCB production.
  • Integrating electronic designs with physical components and ensuring compatibility.

PCB Project Documentation Using Altium

In this project, we designed and built a PCB from scratch
using Altium Designer. The process involved creating the
schematic, selecting components, and fabricating the board.

1. PCB Design in Altium

We started by designing the PCB layout in Altium. The design
included setting up layers, routing traces, and ensuring
that all components were properly placed. Design rules were
also configured to meet manufacturing requirements.

2. Component Selection from LCSC

After finalizing the PCB design, we selected the necessary
components from LCSC. These components were chosen for their
compatibility with our design and were ordered for assembly.

3. PCB Fabrication

Once the design and components were ready, the PCB was sent
for fabrication. The board was manufactured based on our
custom design, and the traces and layers were implemented accurately

4. Assembly of the IR Sensor

We integrated an IR sensor into the PCB. The IR sensor
was connected to the design, and its functionality was
tested for accurate signal detection and transmission.

Design Finishes

5. Soldering and Final Assembly

After receiving the fabricated PCB, we soldered the
components, including the IR sensor and others from LCSC.
Proper soldering techniques were used to ensure reliable connections.

This project allowed us to gain hands-on experience in
designing and building a complete PCB solution.
